03 — ENERGY MANAGEMENT

ENERVISION PROJECT
KIGALI 2030

This study outlines a path to 90% energy self-sufficiency for Kigali's Nyarugenge District by 2050. It compares three investment pathways and highlights the ambitious "Delulu" scenario, which cuts transport emissions by 56% through e-mobility and a tram system. It replaces firewood with district-wide biogas and shifts the grid to 88.8% renewables, mainly rooftop solar and agrivoltaics. Despite higher upfront costs, it lowers long-term electricity costs (0.178 vs 0.239 USD/kWh) and improves environmental and social resilience.

04 — CSP–PV hybrid plant

CSP–PV Hybrid Plant
in Spain

The report presents a techno-economic pre-feasibility study of a CSP–PV hybrid plant in southern Spain, using a Genetic Algorithm to identify an optimal design with 142 MWdc PV, 50 MWel CSP, and 8 hours of thermal storage. The system achieves a base price of 52.59 USD/MWh and an LCOE of 75.70 USD/MWh while meeting grid and dispatch constraints, and it also supports sustainability goals and local job creation.

05 — SAMBU ISLAND, INDONESIA

Submarine Cable Project De-Dieselization

As part of PLN Riau Islands’ planning team, I helped develop a solution to replace Pertamina Sambu’s 3.45 MW diesel plant with cleaner electricity from Batam via a submarine cable, which began operating on 15 April 2025. The project delivers about Rp 58 billion (€3.4 million) in annual savings and reduces emissions by around 1,090 t CO₂ per year, demonstrating how island interconnection can lower costs, improve reliability, and support Indonesia’s energy transition.
